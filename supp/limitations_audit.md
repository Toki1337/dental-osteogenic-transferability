|---|---|---|---|

| 2 | **Per-dataset n is small** | n = 4–6 per dataset; GSE99958 control n=1. | RRA uses rankings (robust to n); LOO stability is the key guard; ALPL/BMP2/DLX5/MSX2 recovered. |

| 4 | **In-vitro separable; does NOT transfer in vivo** | Separable in independent in-vitro PDLF (GSE316449, AUC=1.0, underpowered) but does NOT transfer to in-vivo sorted progenitors (GSE104473, ρ=−0.14) NOR to human MRONJ mesenchyme (GSE303003: program not suppressed, sign test 43.9%; no osteoprogenitor home). | Two independent in-vivo contexts converge on non-transfer; reported as the load-bearing boundary, not hidden. |
| 5 | **Failure side now human + replicated, but control is inflammatory** | PRIMARY failure analysis = GSE303003 (human, 4 MRONJ vs 4 cyst, 65,454 cells): replicated osteoclast/myeloid expansion (p=0.029) but program NOT suppressed and lacking osteoprogenitor home. Control = cyst granuloma (inflammatory, not healthy bone). | Projection restricted to mesenchymal compartment; **pre-specified** whole-program sign test (not curated subset). | 12 | **Multi-signature benchmark — k/N transfer (not universal)** | Across 13 signatures + positive control on **3 jaw-disease scRNA atlases** (human MRONJ GSE303003, mouse BRONJ GSE295106, mouse ORNJ GSE269255): prior-knowledge osteo sets + canonical markers localise to in-vivo osteoprogenitors in ALL three (4/4), data-driven in-vitro signatures incl. | 13 | **Culture-state confound (now quantified)** | RRA-744 ≈19% culture programs (hypoxia p=6e-13, serum p=5e-7, interferon, senescence); the data-driven panel is ≈5% canonical osteo-identity genes vs ≈92% for curated sets. | Disclosed as the gene-set-level mechanism for non-transfer. Culture load **alone** does not predict transfer (Spearman ρ=0.20, ns); the gap is joint osteo-identity depletion + induction-context capture. |
| 14 | **Ortholog-mapping fix + released tool** | Corrected `utils::to_human_symbols` (babelgene `human=FALSE`; previously mapped only ~15/23k mouse genes), so `run_all.R` reproduces the mouse projections end-to-end; GSE104473 ρ=−0.14 unchanged under correct mapping. Released `transferaudit` R package + vignette + in-vivo-correspondence/STROBE-MR/scRNA checklists. | Strengthens the reproducibility claim (canonical pipeline now maps thousands of orthologs, not 15); load-bearing negative robust to the fix. |

**Bottom line:** the L1 program is real and shows out-of-sample in-vitro separability (GSE316449, underpowered, partly constructive), but does **not** transfer in vivo — now demonstrated on TWO independent contexts: mouse distraction osteogenesis (GSE104473, ρ=−0.14) and replicated human MRONJ single-cell (GSE303003, 4v4: not suppressed, no osteoprogenitor home, hardened by dual scoring + osteo-marker positive control). The human failure side validates that the data capture real MRONJ pathology (osteoclast/myeloid expansion, p=0.029) while refuting the suppression hypothesis. MR (corrected lead-SNP + IVW/Egger) nominates no robust druggable target; FinnGen osteoporosis gives weak directional concordance only. We avoid "externally/biologically validated"; the program is **internally consistent and in-vitro separable**, with its in-vivo non-correspondence as the central, human-anchored honest finding. Every limitation above is stated in the manuscript Discussion.
